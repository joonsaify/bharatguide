How to Upload Documents in DigiLocker – Step by Step Guide

📅 Updated: 2026-06-10 · ⏱ 4 min

While government-issued documents in DigiLocker are the most valuable, you can also upload your own documents (self-uploaded). This guide shows you how to do it step by step.

What You Can Upload

You can store digital copies of any document in your DigiLocker. Useful for:

  • College ID cards
  • Previous marksheets not in the Issued Documents system
  • Experience certificates
  • Achievement certificates
  • Medical reports and prescriptions
  • Property documents (sale deed, registry)

Step-by-Step Process

Open DigiLockerOpen the DigiLocker app or go to https://digilocker.gov.in and log in with your Aadhaar and PIN.
Go to 'Uploaded Documents'In the dashboard, select 'Uploaded Documents' section (different from Issued Documents). This is where your self-uploaded files live.
Click '+' or 'Upload'Click the upload button. Select the document category (Academic, Medical, Identity, etc.) or create a custom label.
Select FileChoose a PDF or image file from your phone/computer. Maximum file size is 10MB per document. Supported formats: PDF, JPG, PNG.
Add Document NameGive your document a clear name (e.g., 'B.Tech Semester 4 Marksheet'). This makes finding it later easy.
SubmitClick upload. Your document is now stored in DigiLocker and accessible from any device.
💡 Tip:Self-uploaded documents are not 'verified' by any authority. For official verification, use the 'Issued Documents' section. However, many colleges and employers accept self-uploaded DigiLocker documents for internal purposes.
