How to Verify Documents on DigiLocker – QR Code & URI Verification Guide

📅 Updated: 2026-06-09 · ⏱ 3 min

One of the best features of DigiLocker is document verification. Employers, colleges, and government offices can instantly verify your documents without seeing the original. Here is how verification works.

What Makes DigiLocker Documents Verifiable?

Documents issued through DigiLocker's 'Issued Documents' section are digitally signed by the issuing authority (CBSE, MP Board, etc.). Each document has a unique QR code and a URI (Uniform Resource Identifier) that points to the verified copy on DigiLocker servers.

Two Ways to Share Verified Documents

1. QR Code (Digital or Printed)

Each document in DigiLocker has a QR code at the top. You can:

  • Show the QR code on your phone screen — verifier scans it with any QR scanner
  • Print the document — the QR code on the printout can still be scanned
  • The QR code opens a secure verification page on DigiLocker showing the original document

2. Share URI Link

Each document has a unique URI (a long URL). You can:

  • Click 'Share' and send the URI via email or WhatsApp
  • The verifier opens the link in any browser — it shows the official verified copy
  • No login required for the verifier. The link works for 30 days.

What Verifiers See

When someone scans your QR code or opens the URI, they see: the exact document as issued by the authority, document issue date and issuer details, digital signature verification status (✓ Verified), and tamper detection (shows if document was modified).

💡 Tip:DigiLocker documents are recognized as legally valid under the IT Act, 2000. Most Indian universities, government exams (SSC, UPSC), and private employers accept DigiLocker verified documents without needing physical copies.
